Library Subject Guide: Implicit Bias

1. The Implicit Association Test (IAT)

The IAT is a tool developed by Harvard researchers to measure the strength of associations between concepts (e.g., race, gender) and evaluations (e.g., good, bad). It reveals biases that exist below our conscious awareness.

Try it yourself: Take the Project Implicit Test

Critique & Context

While the IAT is a landmark tool, it is important to remember it is a snapshot, not a permanent diagnosis. Scholars suggest using it as a starting point for self-reflection rather than a definitive measure of character.

2. Intersectionality

Coined by Professor Kimberlé Crenshaw, intersectionality describes how various social identities (race, class, gender, sexuality) overlap to create unique modes of discrimination or privilege.

Video: Kimberlé Crenshaw explains the urgency of intersectionality.

3. The Coded Gaze: Algorithmic Bias

Computer scientist Joy Buolamwini discovered that facial recognition software often fails to detect dark-skinned faces accurately. This is much more than a glitch, as it reveals an example of "coded gaze" in which the bias of the programmers is revealed through the use of technologies.

Video: Joy Buolamwini demonstrates the "Coded Gaze" in facial recognition.

The Power Shift: Buolamwini founded the Algorithmic Justice League. This organzation shows that code can be used as a tool for advocacy, not just oppression.
